David Attenborough

Even at 94 years old, Sir David Attenborough is still working tirelessly to educate audiences about nature and why we must protect it.

Biography:

  • 1926 - Born in Isleworth, England, David spent much of his childhood not just close to nature but also in it, collecting fossils, stones and natural specimens.

  • 1936 - Attended a lecture by Grey Owl at De Montfort Hall, Leicester, which would influence David's unflinching advocacy for conservation.

  • 1945 - Won a scholarship to Clare College, Cambridge, where he studied geology and zoology, and obtained a degree in natural sciences.

  • 1947 - Was called up for national service in the Royal Navy. He would spend two years stationed in North Wales and the Firth of Forth.

  • 1950 - Married Jane Elizabeth Ebsworth Oriel. The couple had two children.

  • 1952 - Joined the BBC full-time as a producer.

  • 1954 - Became the presenter for the programme Zoo Quest and worked as a producer on the Travellers' Tales and Adventure series.

  • 1960 - Resigned from BBC's permanent staff to study for a postgraduate degree in social antrophology, while continuing to film.

  • 1965 - Returned to BBC full time as a controller, but thanks to a clause in his contract he was able to continue making programmes occasionally.

  • 1969 - Made a three-part series on the cultural history of Bali. He was promoted to director of programmes that same year.

  • 1971 - Joined the first Western expedition that would seek out a lost tribe in a remote highland valley in New Guinea.

  • 1973 - Left his position as director of programmes and returned to full-time programme-making. This resulted in the series Eastwards with Attenborough which aired the same year.

  • 1975 - Was the presenter on the series Tribal Eye and The Explorers.

  • 1976 - Began production on Life on Earth.

  • 1979 - Beginning with Life on Earth, David set about creating a body of work which became a benchmark of quality in wildlife film-making.

  • 1984 - Wrote and presented the series The Living Planet.

  • 1985 - Was knighted by Queen Elizabeth.

  • 1987 - Wrote and presented The First Eden, a series about man's influence on the natural history of the Mediterranean basin.

  • 1989 - Wrote and presented Lost Worlds, Vanished Lives, a four part documentary concerning the discovery of fossils.

  • 1990 - Wrote and presented The Trials of Life, the final part of the Lifetrilogy.

  • 1993 - Wrote and presented Life in the Freezer, a television series about the natural history of Antarctica.

  • 1995 - Wrote and presented The Private Life of Plants, for which he won his first Peabody Award the following year.

  • 1998 - Wrote and presented The Life of Birds, for which he won his second Peabody Award.

  • 2000 - Wrote and presented State of the Planet. a three-part environmental documentary series about the impact of man's activities on the natural world..

  • 2001 - Narrated the documentary series The Blue Planet.

  • 2002 - Wrote and presented The Life of Mammals.

  • 2005 - Wrote and presented Life in the Undergrowth, which introduced audiences to the world of invertebrates. During the year, Attenborough was appointed patron of the UK's Blood Pressure Association.

  • 2006 - Narrated Planet Earth, the biggest nature documentary ever made for television. He was also named the most trusted celebrity in the UK.

  • 2007 - Won The Culture Show's Living Icon Award.

  • 2008 - Wrote and presented Life in Cold Blood, which centered around reptiles and amphibians.

  • 2009 - Co-wrote and narrated Life, a ten-part series focusing on extraordinary animal behaviour. He also narrated Nature's Great Events, played the role of a floor polisher in "A Grand, Grand Overture" that same year and became a patron of Population Matters, a UK charity advocating sustainable human populations.

  • 2011 - Presented Frozen Planet, a major series on the natural history of the polar regions.

  • 2013 - At this point, Attenborough had collected 32 honorary degrees from British universities, more than any other person.

  • 2015 - Narrated The Great Barrier Reef, a three-part documentary series about life in the Great Barrier Reef.

  • 2016 - Narrated and presented Planet Earth II. In May, the British polar research ship RRS Sir David Attenborough was named in his honour.

  • 2017 - Narrated and presented Blue Planet II, a documentary series about marine life.

  • 2018 - Narrated the five-part series Dynasties.

  • 2019 - Narrated the eight-part series Our Planet, which addresses issues of conservation.

  • 2020 - Wrote and presented David Attenborough: A Life on Our Planet, which he considers his personal witness statement of his life and the future. He was also awarded his second knighthood and appointed a Knight Grand Cross of the Order of St Michael and St George for services to television and conservation.

“It seems to me that the natural world is the greatest source of excitement; the greatest source of visual beauty; the greatest source of intellectual interest. It is the greatest source of so much in life that makes life worth living.”
- Sir David Attenborough
